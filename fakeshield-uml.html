§ 1
Actor Identification
▾
Primary · External 🧑‍💻
Citizen / Reporter
Any Indian citizen who suspects a fake social media account. Submits reports via the public portal or browser extension. Does not require system login. Represents the public-facing input to the system.
Primary · Internal 👮
Agency Officer (Analyst)
Authenticated officer of the Central Agency (e.g., CERT-In / MeitY Cyber Cell). Reviews flagged accounts, verifies AI results, manages cases, and sends takedown requests to platforms.
Primary · Internal 👑
Senior Officer / Admin
Supervises the agency dashboard, manages officer accounts and roles, reviews analytics, escalates high-profile cases to law enforcement, and generates official legal reports for court proceedings.
Secondary · External 🏛️
Law Enforcement Agency
Cyber Crime Cell / Police / CBI. Receives escalated cases involving serious offences (fraud, impersonation at scale, political interference). Acts on blockchain-backed legal reports.
Secondary · External 🌐
Social Media Platform
Meta (Facebook/Instagram), Twitter/X, YouTube. Receives formal takedown requests from the agency. Must respond within SLA window (36–72 hrs under IT Rules 2021). Sends deletion/rejection confirmations back.
System · Internal 🤖
AI Detection Engine
Automated system actor. Runs ML pipelines: metadata classifier, image deepfake detector, NLP analyzer, and GNN graph analyzer. Produces FakeScore (0–100) and SHAP explainability breakdown. Operates continuously.
System · Internal ⛓️
Blockchain Network
Hyperledger Fabric permissioned blockchain. System actor that stores tamper-proof evidence hashes and case lifecycle transactions. Provides immutable audit trail for legal admissibility. Operates via smart contracts.

Use Case IDUC-01
NameSubmit Fake Account Report
Actor(s)Primary: Citizen / Reporter | Secondary: AI Detection Engine
DescriptionAny citizen who suspects a social media account to be fake can submit a formal report through the public portal or browser extension, without requiring system login. The system automatically initiates an AI scan of the reported account.
PreconditionsPRE-1 Citizen has access to the public reporting portal or browser extension. PRE-2 The suspected account is accessible on the respective platform.
PostconditionsA unique ticket number is generated. The reported account is queued for AI scanning. An acknowledgment is sent to the reporter.
Main Flow
1
Citizen
Opens the public reporting portal (fakeshield.gov.in/report) or clicks "Report to Agency" in the browser extension.
2
Citizen
Fills the report form: selects platform (Facebook / Instagram / Twitter), enters the suspicious account URL or @username, selects reason for suspicion (Impersonation / Financial Fraud / Political Misuse / Bot / Other), and optionally uploads screenshots.
3
Citizen
Submits the form. System validates the URL format and checks for duplicate reports on the same account.
4
System
Generates a unique ticket number (e.g., FS-2024-00847) and stores the report in the database with status = "Received".
5
System
Sends an SMS/email acknowledgment to the reporter with the ticket number and expected processing timeline (48–72 hrs).
6
AI Detection Engine
Automatically initiates a full AI scan on the reported account (triggers UC-05). Scan results are linked to the report.
7
System
If FakeScore > 70, the report is auto-escalated to the agency dashboard as a new case. If score is between 40–70, placed in review queue. Below 40, flagged as low priority.
Alternate Flows
ALT-A: Duplicate Report — If the account has already been reported, the new report is merged into the existing case and the reporter is informed with the existing ticket number.
ALT-B: Invalid URL — If the URL is malformed or the account is no longer accessible, the system notifies the reporter and asks for correction.
ExceptionsPlatform API is unavailable → Report is saved, and scan is scheduled for retry within 30 minutes. Reporter is not notified of the API issue.
Includes«extend» UC-02 Citizen may subsequently track the report status using the ticket number.

Use Case IDUC-04
NameScan Account Manually
Actor(s)Primary: Agency Officer | System: AI Detection Engine
DescriptionAn authenticated officer (or citizen via browser extension) can manually trigger a full AI-powered scan on any social media account by providing the profile URL or username. The system runs all ML models and returns a comprehensive FakeScore with evidence breakdown.
PreconditionsPRE-1 Officer is authenticated (UC-03). PRE-2 Platform API keys are configured and active.
PostconditionsAccount is scanned, FakeScore is computed, and results are stored. If score > 70, account is added to the flagged list and a new case is created.
Main Flow
1
Officer
Navigates to "Scan Account" page and enters the profile URL (e.g., https://twitter.com/suspected_user) or @username, and selects the platform.
2
Officer
Clicks "Run Full Scan". System displays a progress indicator with live status updates (Metadata → Image → NLP → Graph).
3
System
Calls the Detection Service → triggers UC-05 (Auto-Detect). Fetches account data via the platform's API.
4
AI Detection Engine
Executes all four ML pipelines in parallel: (a) Metadata Classifier, (b) Image Deepfake Detector, (c) NLP Bio/Post Analyzer, (d) Graph Neural Network analyzer.
5
System
Aggregates sub-scores into a final FakeScore (0–100) and generates a SHAP explanation identifying top contributing features.
6
System
Displays results via UC-06 (View Detection Results).
7
System
If FakeScore ≥ 70: creates a new Case record (status = "New"), prompts officer to review. Auto-notifies on-duty senior officer for scores ≥ 90.
Alternate Flows
ALT-A: Private/Protected Account — System can only analyze publicly available metadata. Image and NLP pipelines may return partial results. Officer is warned that score confidence is reduced.
ALT-B: API Rate Limit Reached — Scan is queued for up to 10 minutes and officer is notified to wait.
Includes«include» UC-05 Auto-Detect Fake Account — mandatory part of every scan. «include» UC-06 View Detection Results — automatically shown after scan.
Use Case IDUC-05
NameAuto-Detect Fake Account [AI Engine]
Actor(s)System Actor: AI Detection Engine (included by UC-04)
DescriptionThe automated AI detection engine executes four parallel ML pipelines on an account and produces a composite FakeScore with confidence levels. This is a system use case triggered internally — not directly initiated by a human actor.
PreconditionsPRE-1 Account metadata and/or public posts are fetchable. PRE-2 All ML model files are loaded and healthy.
PostconditionsFakeScore (0–100), sub-scores for each pipeline, and SHAP top-3 feature explanations are stored and returned.
Main Flow
1
AI Engine
Pipeline A — Metadata Analysis: Extracts 40+ features: account age, follower/following ratio, post frequency, username entropy score, verification status, profile picture presence, bio completeness, link in bio, historical name changes. XGBoost classifier returns metadata_score.
2
AI Engine
Pipeline B — Image Analysis: Downloads profile picture. Runs DeepFace for face detection. Applies FaceForensics++ model to detect AI-generated (GAN) or face-swapped images. ResNet-50 computes image_authenticity_score.
3
AI Engine
Pipeline C — NLP Analysis: Tokenizes bio and last 20 posts using multilingual BERT. Detects spam language, copy-pasted content, keyword stuffing, incoherence, and suspicious URLs. Computes nlp_score. Supports Hindi, Hinglish, Tamil, Bengali.
4
AI Engine
Pipeline D — Graph Analysis: Constructs a follower/following network using NetworkX. Runs Graph Neural Network (PyTorch Geometric) to detect bot clusters, inflated follower counts, and co-follower anomalies. Computes graph_score.
5
AI Engine
Scorer module applies weighted aggregation: FakeScore = 0.30×metadata + 0.25×image + 0.25×nlp + 0.20×graph. Confidence interval is computed from model certainty.
6
AI Engine
SHAP explainer identifies top 3 features driving the score (e.g., "Account created 3 days ago", "Profile image detected as AI-generated", "83% posts are copy-pasted content").
7
AI Engine
Returns structured JSON result to the Detection Service. Total execution time target: ≤ 25 seconds.
Alternate Flows
ALT-A: One pipeline fails (e.g., image download error) → Remaining pipelines run; score is computed from available data with reduced weight. Confidence label changes to "Partial".

PreconditionsPRE-1 Case exists in "New" or "Under Review" status. PRE-2 AI scan results are available (UC-06).

Officer

2
Officer
Reviews AI detection results via UC-06 (included). Examines all evidence panels and SHAP explanations.
3
Officer
Manually cross-checks the suspected account against the official/verified account of the impersonated entity using links stored in the FakeShield verified entity database.
4
Officer
Adds internal review notes (e.g., "Profile photo verified as AI-generated via reverse image search. Bio matches politician X's official page verbatim.").
5
Officer
Updates case classification: Confirmed Fake / False Positive / Needs More Information / Refer to Senior Officer.
6
System
On "Confirmed Fake": triggers UC-11 (Lock Evidence on Blockchain) and moves case to "Takedown Ready" queue.
7
Officer
Proceeds to UC-08 (Send Takedown Request) or escalates to senior officer via UC-12.

Use Case IDUC-08
NameSend Takedown Request
Actor(s)Primary: Agency Officer | External: Social Media Platform
DescriptionAfter confirming a fake account, the officer sends a formal, structured takedown request to the respective social media platform via the system. The request follows a legally formatted template under IT Rules 2021 and is tracked for SLA compliance.
PreconditionsPRE-1 Case is in "Takedown Ready" status (confirmed fake via UC-07). PRE-2 Platform contact details are configured in the system. PRE-3 Evidence is locked on blockchain (UC-11).
PostconditionsTakedown request is sent and logged. SLA countdown begins. Blockchain transaction records the takedown submission.
Main Flow
1
Officer
Clicks "Generate Takedown Request" on the confirmed case.
2
System
Auto-generates a structured takedown request document including: case ID, blockchain evidence hash, FakeScore, officer ID, legal basis (IT Act Section 66C/66D, IT Rules 2021), platform account URL, and requested action (Delete / Suspend / Review).
3
Officer
Reviews and digitally approves the document. Senior officer counter-approval required if FakeScore < 80 or for politically sensitive cases.
4
System
Sends the request via API/email to the platform's registered Grievance Officer endpoint. Marks case status as "Takedown Requested" with timestamp.
5
System
Starts SLA countdown timer based on platform type: Meta = 36 hrs, Twitter = 24 hrs, YouTube = 72 hrs (per IT Rules 2021).
6
Blockchain
Records "TAKEDOWN_SENT" transaction with timestamp, officer ID, and request hash on Hyperledger Fabric.
7
System
Sends automated reminder to platform at 50% and 90% of SLA window. Platform's response is tracked via UC-09.
Alternate Flows
ALT-A: Senior officer rejects approval → Case is returned to officer with rejection notes. Must be re-reviewed.
Includes«include» UC-11 Evidence must be locked on blockchain before sending request.
Extended by«extend» UC-12 Escalate to Law Enforcement — if platform fails to comply or case severity warrants it.
Use Case IDUC-09
NameRespond to Takedown Request
Actor(s)Primary: Social Media Platform (external)
DescriptionThe social media platform (Meta, Twitter, YouTube, etc.) receives the formal takedown request and responds within the SLA window. Their response (Account Deleted / Suspended / Rejected with Reason) is recorded in the system and on the blockchain.
PreconditionsPRE-1 Takedown request has been sent via UC-08. PRE-2 Platform has a registered API endpoint or email contact in the system.
PostconditionsPlatform response is recorded. SLA compliance status is updated. Blockchain is updated. Case is closed or escalated.
Main Flow
1
Platform
Receives the takedown request via API callback or email. Reviews the case documentation and evidence hash.
2
Platform
Conducts internal review per their community standards and Terms of Service against the provided evidence.
3
Platform
Sends response to FakeShield system: (a) Complied — Account Deleted, (b) Complied — Account Suspended, (c) Rejected — Insufficient Evidence, (d) Rejected — Account Complies with Policy.
4
System
Records response with timestamp. Calculates actual response time vs SLA window. Updates SLA compliance metrics.
5
Blockchain
Records "PLATFORM_RESPONSE" transaction with outcome code and timestamp on Hyperledger Fabric.
6
System
If complied: case is closed, reporter is notified. If rejected: case is flagged for senior officer review with rejection reason. If no response within SLA: triggers auto-escalation alert.
Alternate Flows
ALT-A: SLA Breached (No Response) — System auto-escalates the case, notifies the senior officer, and generates a non-compliance report under IT Rules 2021 (intermediary liability — Section 79).
ALT-B: Partial Action (Account Suspended but not Deleted) — Officer reviews and decides whether this satisfies the request or if further action is needed.

Main Flow
1
Officer
Opens "Platform SLA" section from the dashboard.
2
System
Displays per-platform compliance dashboard: response rate (%), average response time, pending requests, overdue cases (color-coded red/yellow/green).
3
System

Use Case IDUC-11
NameLock Evidence on Blockchain
Actor(s)System Actor: Blockchain Network (Hyperledger Fabric)
DescriptionWhen a case is confirmed as fake (UC-07) or a takedown is sent (UC-08), the system computes a SHA-256 hash of the entire evidence bundle and submits it as an immutable transaction to the Hyperledger Fabric blockchain. This creates a tamper-proof legal record.
PreconditionsPRE-1 Case has been confirmed or takedown is being sent. PRE-2 Fabric network is operational. PRE-3 Signing certificate for the agency node is valid.
PostconditionsImmutable blockchain transaction ID is stored in the case record. Evidence is legally auditable.
Main Flow
1
System
Assembles the evidence bundle: account metadata JSON, profile screenshot (base64), AI score breakdown, officer review notes, platform response (if any), and case timeline.
2
System
Computes SHA-256 hash of the serialized evidence bundle. Stores the hash string in the case record's evidence_hash field.
3
Blockchain Network
Submits a transaction to the Evidence Smart Contract (evidence_contract.go) on Hyperledger Fabric with: {case_id, evidence_hash, actor_id, timestamp, event_type}.
4
Blockchain Network
Fabric endorses the transaction across peer nodes (agency node + CERT-In observer node), commits to the ledger, and returns a transaction ID (TX_ID).
5
System
Stores TX_ID in the case record. All subsequent case state changes (Takedown Sent, Platform Responded, Escalated, Closed) are also recorded as blockchain transactions via the SLA smart contract.
6
Officer
Can view the full blockchain audit trail from the case page, showing every transaction hash and timestamp in chronological order.
Alternate Flows
ALT-A: Fabric network unavailable → Evidence locking is queued with a retry mechanism (3 retries in 5-minute intervals). Case cannot move to "Takedown Ready" until blockchain lock succeeds.
Use Case IDUC-12
NameEscalate to Law Enforcement
Actor(s)Primary: Senior Officer | External: Law Enforcement Agency
DescriptionWhen a fake account case involves serious criminal activity (financial fraud, targeted harassment, political interference, impersonation at scale) OR the platform fails to comply within the SLA, the senior officer escalates the case to the Cyber Crime Cell or CBI with a complete legal dossier backed by the blockchain audit trail.
PreconditionsPRE-1 Case has been confirmed fake (UC-07). PRE-2 Evidence is locked on blockchain (UC-11). PRE-3 Either: (a) SLA has been breached by platform, or (b) case meets severity threshold for criminal action.
PostconditionsFormal legal dossier sent to law enforcement. Case status updated to "Escalated". Blockchain records the escalation event.
Main Flow
1
Senior Officer
Reviews the case and determines escalation criteria are met (SLA breach, or criminal severity ≥ Level 2 in the internal classification matrix).
2
System
Auto-generates a complete legal dossier PDF including: case summary, AI evidence, officer review notes, blockchain TX history, platform non-compliance record, applicable IPC/BNS sections (66C, 66D, 419, 420, 500 BNS 2023).
3
Senior Officer
Digitally signs and approves the legal dossier with their PKI-based digital signature.
4
System
Transmits the dossier to the registered Cyber Crime Cell via secure encrypted channel (SFTP or NIC-secured API).
5
Law Enforcement
Receives and acknowledges receipt of the dossier. Provides a reference FIR number or acknowledgment code.
6
Blockchain
Records "CASE_ESCALATED" transaction with law enforcement reference ID.
7
System
Updates case status to "Escalated". Notifies the original reporter (if applicable) that case has been elevated.
Extended by«extend» of UC-08 — Escalation is an optional but possible extension whenever a takedown has been attempted and failed or criminal conduct is confirmed.
